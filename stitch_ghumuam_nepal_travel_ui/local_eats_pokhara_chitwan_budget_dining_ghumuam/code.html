Culinary Guide

Taste the
Himalayas

Navigating Nepal’s food scene requires understanding the 'dual-pricing reality'. While tourist hubs offer inflated menus, authentic and budget-friendly meals lie hidden in plain sight. Embrace the 'Tea Shop' strategy: look for modest, locally-filled eateries where Dal Bhat is plentiful and momos are handmade.

Culinary Insights

visibility_off Shadow Costs

Beware of hidden charges in tourist hubs. Menus often omit the mandatory 10% service charge and 13% VAT, inflating your bill by nearly a quarter. Always ask if prices are inclusive of taxes before ordering, especially in places like Thamel or Lakeside.

swap_horiz Strategic Substitutions

Swap imported goods for local staples. Instead of expensive espresso, opt for rich Chiya (Nepali tea). Trade Western-style bakeries for traditional Sel Roti stands to save money and experience authentic local flavors that fuel the Himalayas.
